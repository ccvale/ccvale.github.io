My name is Chris Valente, a third year Computer Science major at Ramapo College, who is also minoring in Data Science, with a 3.65 GPA. I love all things sports and technology, and am also extremely passionate about my two main hobbies; writing and music. I am obsessed with the concept of creating things, and am the kind of person who would rather spend hours to automate a simple task than to do the task itself; for better or for worse.

In that facet, I've written a lot of fun scripts ranging in complexity from using web-scraping to compile a daily schedule of relevant sporting events, to implementing song recognition APIs to change the color of my lights to match the colors of the album cover of a song it hears. In terms of larger projects, my favorite project I've created so far is called babyGENIUS, a desktop application that mimics a removed feature from the GENIUS phone app, which quickly detected and fetched lyrics of a song that you're currently listening to on your phone. My version connects to the users Spotify and mimics that functionality, as well as having the option to search independently for songs and queuing them to be played on whichever device you're currently using Spotify.

In my semesters as a student, I've taken many relevant courses where I learned a wide range of fundamentals, such as data structures, how to manipulate and visualize data in Python and R using many of their key libraries, as well as being trained and well equipped with knowledge on industry standard tools like Git, VSCode, testing suites, and more.

Also, I have most recently given presentations on research I have done regarding the role of technology in sports (specifically in how it affects the players, the fans, and how it changes the game analytically), and have also worked on linear/multiple regression models as a part of a final project report with the goal of understanding and predicting what songs are likely to chart the billboards and why.
